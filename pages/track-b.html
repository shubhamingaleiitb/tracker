B1. Working-Level Math for ML Months 1–4

Your Engineering Physics background gives you calculus and linear algebra. Research-level ML needs specific mathematical fluency. The goal: follow a paper's derivation and understand why the authors made specific choices.

Resources

ResourceTypeWhy It Matters
Mathematics for Machine Learning (Deisenroth) Book Free online; chapters 5–7 on optimization and probability
Information Theory, Inference and Learning (MacKay) Book Chapters 1–6; deep intuition for information theory
Boyd's Convex Optimization (Stanford) Lectures First 8 lectures; understand the optimization landscape
3Blue1Brown (YouTube) Video Visual intuition for abstract concepts

What to Develop

  • Optimization: Convex vs non-convex, gradient descent variants, convergence guarantees MML Ch. 7
  • Understand why Adam works and when it doesn't Adam Paper
  • KL divergence — not just as loss, but what does it actually measure? MacKay Ch. 2
  • Entropy, mutual information — appears everywhere in RLHF, VAEs, diffusion MacKay Ch. 1–4
  • Eigendecomposition, SVD, matrix approximations — why LoRA works (low-rank structure) MML Ch. 4
  • Variational methods: ELBO, variational inference basics VAE Paper
  • Watch Boyd's first 8 Convex Optimization lectures YouTube Playlist
  • Read MML Chapters 5–7 (Vector Calculus, Probability, Optimization) MML Book
  • Litmus test: Read the DPO paper derivation (Section 3) and follow every step DPO Paper

B3. Understanding Model Training at Depth Months 4–8

You've fine-tuned models — now understand the training dynamics. Why do certain hyperparameters matter? What's actually happening during LoRA fine-tuning at a gradient level?

Resources

ResourceTypeWhy It Matters
Karpathy's Neural Networks: Zero to Hero Video Build everything from scratch; the gold standard
Karpathy's nanoGPT Code Read every line; go deeper than your char-level version
Chinchilla Paper Paper Scaling laws; understand compute-optimal training
The Bitter Lesson (Rich Sutton) Essay Fundamental perspective on what works in AI long-term

What to Understand Deeply

  • Complete Karpathy's Zero to Hero series (all videos) Series
  • Read every line of nanoGPT, understand design decisions GitHub
  • Loss landscapes and optimization dynamics — why does training sometimes diverge?
  • Learning rate schedules and warmup — not just which to use, but WHY they work
  • Gradient accumulation, mixed precision training — the systems side of training
  • Scaling laws — how model size, data, and compute relate Chinchilla
  • What LoRA actually does to the gradient flow — connect practical experience to theory
  • Read The Bitter Lesson and reflect on its implications Essay
